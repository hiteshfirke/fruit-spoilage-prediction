Model & Research Architecture

Dataset summary, training pipeline, and reviewer-focused methodology for the Fruit Spoilage Prediction System.

System Overview

4 ripeness stages 10k+ images MobileNetV2 backbone
  • Input: Banana image + ambient temperature + humidity.
  • Vision head: CNN classifier → ripeness stage.
  • Fusion head: Rule-based spoilage model → days remaining.
  • Output: Stage label + confidence + estimated shelf life.

Pipeline Timeline

    Mix of open-source + captured images across stages, lighting & backgrounds.

  2. Pre-processing

    Resize to 224×224, apply MobileNetV2 preprocessing & heavy augmentation.

  3. Training

    Frozen backbone, dense head with Adam + class-weighted loss & early stopping.

  4. Deployment

Model & Dataset Summary

92.6% test accuracy
Ripeness Stages 4
Training Images 10k+
Backbone MobileNetV2
  • Backbone: MobileNetV2 pre-trained on ImageNet, used as a frozen feature extractor.
  • Task: 4-class banana ripeness classification – stage0_unripe, stage1_ripe, stage2_overripe, stage3_rotten.
  • Dataset: Mix of curated open-source images plus in-house captures across multiple backgrounds, camera angles, and lighting conditions.
  • Augmentation: random flip, rotation, zoom, brightness & contrast jitter, simulating real-world conditions (side views, bunches, partial occlusion).
  • Class imbalance: handled via computed class weights per category in the loss function.
  • Evaluation: final MobileNetV2 head achieves ≈92.6% accuracy on a held-out test set.

Vision output (ripeness stage) is later fused with an environment-based spoilage model (temperature & humidity) to estimate days remaining.

Methodology (For Reviewers)

Reproducibility Notes

  1. 1. Image acquisition

    Curated dataset + in-house captures of bananas at different ripening stages, with variation in background, distance, device, and lighting. Includes both single fruit and small bunch scenarios.

  2. 2. Pre-processing

    Resize to 224×224, apply MobileNetV2 preprocessing, and strong data augmentation (flip, rotation, zoom, brightness/contrast) to improve generalisation to noisy phone photos.

  3. 3. Training

    Freeze the MobileNetV2 backbone, train a dense classification head using Adam, early stopping on validation loss, and class-weighted categorical cross-entropy to balance under-represented stages.

  4. 4. Post-processing

    Brightness-based heuristic distinguishes “dark background” vs “truly black / rotten banana” for borderline predictions, avoiding false “rotten” on under-exposed images.

  5. 5. Environmental fusion

    A simple biological spoilage model uses ambient temperature & humidity to convert the predicted stage into an estimated days remaining range, based on empirical ripening behaviour.
